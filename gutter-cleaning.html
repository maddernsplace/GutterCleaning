What We Do

What Does Gutter Cleaning Involve?

Gutter cleaning is the thorough removal of all leaves, twigs, dirt, moss, seed pods, bird nests and any other debris that has accumulated in your gutters and downpipes over time.

At Maddern's Place, we don't just scoop out what's visible. We perform a complete clean of your entire gutter system — from the gutters themselves through to every downpipe outlet — then flush the whole system with water to confirm it's flowing freely before we leave.

We also do a visual inspection of your gutters while we're up there, letting you know of any damage, rust, sagging, or issues that may need attention.

Why It Matters

What Happens When Gutters Are Left Blocked?

Blocked gutters aren't just an eyesore. Over time they cause serious, costly damage to your home.

Roof & Ceiling Damage

Water that can't drain overflows back under the roof line, causing rot in fascia boards, ceiling leaks, and structural damage that costs thousands to repair.

Foundation & Wall Damage

Overflowing gutters pour water down the side of your home, saturating the soil around foundations and causing subsidence, cracking, and damp internal walls.

Pest Infestations

Debris-filled gutters are a perfect nesting ground for mosquitoes, cockroaches, rats, possums, and birds. Standing water becomes a mosquito breeding site.

Mould & Damp

Persistent moisture from blocked gutters seeps into walls and ceilings, creating the ideal conditions for mould growth — a health hazard for your family.

Garden & Landscaping Damage

Uncontrolled overflow dumps large volumes of water onto garden beds and paths, washing away soil, drowning plants, and eroding landscaping.

Costly Gutter Replacement

The weight of wet, compacted debris warps and cracks gutters over time. Regular cleaning is a small cost compared to full gutter replacement.

⚠️ Warning Signs Your Gutters Need Cleaning

  • Water spilling over the edge during rain

  • Staining or marks on your external walls

  • You've had recent storms or heavy leaf fall
  • Blocked or slow-draining downpipes
  • Rust or corrosion visible from ground level
  • Birds or pests regularly seen on your roof
  • Damp patches appearing on internal ceilings
Do You Need a Clean?

How Do You Know When It's Time?

Many homeowners only notice a gutter problem once water is already getting in somewhere it shouldn't. By that point, damage has often already been done.

The good news is that blocked gutters usually give you warning signs well before serious damage occurs. If you've noticed any of the signs listed here, it's time to book a clean.

As a general rule of thumb, most Australian homes should have their gutters cleaned at least once a year — and twice a year if you have large trees nearby or live in a bushfire-prone area.

Maintenance Guide

How Often Should Gutters Be Cleaned?

The right frequency depends on your property and surroundings.

Once a year

Standard Home

A home with minimal trees nearby and in a low-risk area typically needs a professional clean once per year — ideally in autumn after leaf fall.

Twice a year

Homes Near Trees

If you have large gum trees, pines or deciduous trees close to your roof, twice-yearly cleaning (autumn and spring) is strongly recommended.

3–4 times a year

Bushfire Risk Areas

In high fire-risk zones, debris-filled gutters are a serious fire hazard. Frequent cleaning through summer months is essential for fire safety.

Not sure how often your property needs attention? Contact us — we're happy to assess and recommend the right schedule for your home.
